A Journey of self discovery with fusion of Art and Human Resource

In chemistry, there are great intellectual challenges, and at the same time, there is scientific discovery. However, in pursuing my studies and career, I began to feel that this area of chemistry did not correspond with my deep interest or what I wanted for my career. After much reflection, I decided to explore new avenues that would allow me to merge my skills with a more people-centric approach. This led me to pursue an MBA in HR. The field of HR appealed to me because it's all about people, development, and creating an environment that allows employees to thrive. And I love innovation and creativity.

In HR, I found a natural fit for my strengths in empathy, communication, and patience—qualities I have long recognized as central to my personality. HR is not just about managing organizational processes but about building meaningful relationships, fostering a positive work culture, and ensuring that individuals feel supported and valued.

I am eager to utilize my background and skillset in contributing to organizations that truly value their most valuable asset—people. I am excited about the prospect of being able to help shape environments where people can grow, succeed, and drive organizational success. The experience has been transformative, switching from chemistry to HR, and I look forward to applying those learnings in this dynamic field.

Talking about the creative side that I nurture, I am really good at painting and creating new things with innovation and passion. I love using different colours to create vibrant and captivating paintings that speak for themselves! Painting on a new canvas always feels like starting something fresh and new that brings out my raw emotions and helps me to calm myself while creating. To conclude my part, I hope that I would be able use this wonderful fusion of art and human resource as a subject for benefiting people and organisations that is society as a whole!
